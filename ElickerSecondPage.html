What Are Cover Crops?

Cover crops are a type of crop planted to improve and maintain soil health. They can provide a plethora of benefits when grown such as fixing nutrients in the soil, mitigating erosion, mitigating pests, suppressing weed growth, and providing supplemental income for farmers. If a field is not in use, cover crops are a great way to enrich and maintain your soil's health. However, there are many factors to consider when choosing a blend of cover crops for your land.

Factors to Consider

One important factor to consider when choosing a mix of cover crops is what season you will be growing in. If you are a cotton farmer that plants in the spring and harvests in the fall, it would be advised to plant cover crops in the off seasons, in this case, during the late fall to early spring. It is essential that the cover crops you pick are suited for the season you are growing them in or else they could fail to provide benefits to your soil. For a cotton farmer, picking winter cover crops is advised so that you do not interfere with your growing season.
